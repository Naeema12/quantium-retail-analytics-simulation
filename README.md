##  Project Overview
This project analyzes customer purchasing behavior and evaluates store trial performance in the chip category. 

The goal is to identify key customer segments, understand purchasing patterns, and assess the effectiveness of a store layout trial using data-driven techniques.

---

##  Objectives
- Analyze customer segments and purchasing behavior
- Identify key drivers of sales
- Evaluate store trial performance using A/B testing
- Provide actionable business recommendations

---

##  Key Insights

### Customer Behavior
- Older Families (Budget segment) generate the highest total sales due to higher purchase volume
- Young Singles/Couples (Mainstream) represent the largest customer base
- Mainstream customers show higher spending per unit, indicating impulse purchasing behavior

### Trial Store Performance
- Store 88 showed a significant increase in sales during the trial period
- Store 77 showed no clear improvement
- Store 86 produced inconclusive results

---

##  Analysis Approach

### 1. Data Preparation
- Cleaned and validated transaction and customer datasets
- Created key metrics such as total sales, number of customers, and transactions per customer

### 2. Customer Segmentation
- Analyzed customer groups by life stage and affluence
- Identified high-value segments driving sales

### 3. Experimentation (A/B Testing)
- Selected control stores based on similar pre-trial performance
- Compared trial vs control stores during the trial period
- Measured sales uplift and statistical significance

---

##  Business Recommendations
- Target Older Families and Young Singles/Couples for marketing and promotions
- Apply store layout changes selectively (based on Store 88 results)
- Optimize product placement to encourage impulse purchases
- Improve future testing methods for more reliable insights

---

##  Conclusion
This analysis demonstrates that data-driven decision-making can significantly improve retail performance. While the trial showed strong results in specific stores, its effectiveness varies depending on customer behavior and store characteristics.
